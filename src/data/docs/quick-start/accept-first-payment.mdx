# Accept Your First Payment

Configure payment processing and accept your first client payment with Blawby's integrated payment system.

## Before You Begin

You'll need:
- Active Blawby practice account
- Business bank account information
- Business details (EIN, business address)
- Basic understanding of payment processing

## Step 1: Connect Your Bank Account

### Navigate to Payment Settings
1. Log into your Blawby dashboard
2. Go to **Settings → Payments**
3. Click **Connect Bank Account**

### Enter Banking Information
- **Routing Number**: 9-digit bank routing number
- **Account Number**: Your business bank account number
- **Account Type**: Checking or savings
- **Account Holder**: Business name or your name (for sole proprietors)

### Verification Process
1. Blawby will make two small deposits (under $1.00)
2. Check your bank account in 1-2 business days
3. Enter the deposit amounts in your dashboard
4. Your account will be verified and ready

## Step 2: Configure Payment Methods

### Enable Payment Types
Choose which payment methods to accept:

**Credit/Debit Cards**
- Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover
- Processing fee: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
- Settlement time: 2 business days

**Bank Transfers (ACH)**
- Direct bank-to-bank transfers
- Processing fee: 0.8% per transaction (max $5.00)
- Settlement time: 3-5 business days

**Payment Plans**
- Split large payments over time
- Automatic recurring payments
- Customizable schedules

### Set Processing Limits
Configure limits to manage risk:
- **Maximum single payment**: $10,000 (default)
- **Daily processing limit**: $50,000 (default)
- **Monthly processing limit**: $200,000 (default)

## Step 3: IOLTA Trust Account Setup

### Create Trust Account
If you handle client funds, set up your IOLTA trust account:

1. Go to **Settings → Trust Accounts**
2. Click **Add Trust Account**
3. Enter trust account details:
   - Account number and routing
   - Bank name and address
   - Trust account type (IOLTA/Client Trust)

### Configure Trust Rules
Set up compliance rules:
- **Three-Way Reconciliation**: Monthly reconciliation required
- **Client Fund Segregation**: Separate client from operating funds
- **Audit Trail**: Complete transaction history
- **Reporting**: Monthly trust account reports

### Automated Transfers
Configure automatic transfers:
- **Client to Trust**: Automatically deposit client payments
- **Trust to Operating**: Transfer earned fees to operating account
- **Refund Processing**: Handle client refunds from trust account

## Step 4: Create Your First Invoice

### Basic Invoice
1. Go to **Payments → Create Invoice**
2. Enter client information:
   - Client name and contact details
   - Case matter or description
   - Invoice number (auto-generated)

3. Add line items:
   - **Description**: Legal services provided
   - **Quantity**: Hours or units
   - **Rate**: Hourly rate or flat fee
   - **Amount**: Calculated automatically

4. Set payment terms:
   - **Due Date**: Net 15, Net 30, or custom
   - **Late Fees**: Optional late payment penalties
   - **Payment Methods**: Enabled payment types

### Advanced Invoice Options
- **Recurring Invoices**: Monthly billing arrangements
- **Retainer Invoices**: Initial retainer payments
- **Expense Reimbursement**: Pass-through costs
- **Trust Account Requests**: Payments into trust account

## Step 5: Test Your Payment Setup

### Create Test Invoice
1. Create a test invoice for $1.00
2. Use your own email as the client
3. Send the test invoice

### Test Payment Process
1. Open the test invoice email
2. Click the payment link
3. Enter test payment details:
   - Card number: 4242 4242 4242 4242
   - Expiration: Any future date
   - CVV: Any 3 digits
   - ZIP: Any 5-digit ZIP code

### Verify Payment Processing
1. Check your dashboard for the payment
2. Verify funds appear in your connected bank account
3. Confirm email notifications were sent

## Step 6: Configure Notifications

### Payment Notifications
Set up alerts for payment events:
- **Payment Received**: Instant notification
- **Payment Failed**: Alert for declined payments
- **Invoice Sent**: Confirmation when invoices are sent
- **Overdue Invoices**: Reminder for late payments

### Notification Channels
Choose how to receive notifications:
- **Email**: Primary notification method
- **SMS**: Text message alerts
- **In-App**: Dashboard notifications
- **Webhook**: Custom integrations

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Payment Declined
**Common reasons:**
- Insufficient funds
- Invalid card information
- Fraud detection triggers
- Expired card

**Solutions:**
- Contact client for updated payment information
- Suggest alternative payment method
- Check fraud detection settings

### Bank Account Verification Failed
**Common reasons:**
- Incorrect routing/account numbers
- Account not eligible for ACH
- Bank block on automated transfers

**Solutions:**
- Verify banking information
- Contact bank to allow ACH transfers
- Try different bank account

### Trust Account Issues
**Common reasons:**
- Improper trust account setup
- Missing documentation
- Compliance violations

**Solutions:**
- Review trust account setup
- Submit required documentation
- Contact compliance team
